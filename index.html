About Me
I received the B.S. and M.S. degree from National Central University(NCU), Taiwan, in 2019 and 2021, respectively.
In 2019, I had the opportunity to participate in the YSEP held by the Tokyo Institute of Technology and study in Professor Matsuoka's laboratory. Throughout the program, I successfully completed a research project. Afterward, I was recommended to attend Asian Conference on Remote Sensing(ACRS).
Throughout my master's studies, I dedicated my research to developing algorithms for detecting landslides using bi-temporal satellite imagery. It allowed me to significantly enhance my expertise in computer vision and image analysis.
In my current job, I expose to a wide range of tasks related to image recognition, detection, tracking, camera coordinate transformations, etc. My primary responsibility is to develop a workflow or algorithm that can be deployed on various platforms.
